<p><ul><li>Speech Recognition</li><li>Camera Recognition</li><li>Contact Identification</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Sign Language Translator market encompasses three main types: speech recognition, camera recognition, and contact identification. Speech recognition translates verbal communication into sign language, facilitating real-time interactions. Camera recognition utilizes advanced computer vision algorithms to interpret sign language through video feeds, converting gestures into text or speech. Contact identification focuses on assistive devices that detect physical interactions or gestures, often employing sensors to recognize signs made in close proximity. Together, these technologies enhance communication accessibility for the deaf and hard-of-hearing communities.</p></p>
