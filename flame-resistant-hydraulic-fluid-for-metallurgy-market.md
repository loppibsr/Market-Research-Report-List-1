<p><p>Flame-resistant hydraulic fluids used in metallurgy are categorized into four types based on their composition and properties. </p><p>HFA fluids are aqueous solutions and typically contain water and additives. HFB fluids are also water-containing but incorporate organic solvents for enhanced performance. HFC fluids are primarily water-based with added emulsifiers, offering improved fire resistance. HFD fluids, on the other hand, are non-aqueous, consisting mainly of mineral oils or synthetic compounds, providing superior fire resistance in extreme heat conditions. Each type addresses specific safety and operational needs in metallurgical processes.</p></p>
