<p><p>Para-aramid fibers, known for their high strength and heat resistance, are categorized based on their denier (d), an indication of fiber thickness. The 1000d type is commonly used in applications requiring lightweight and flexible materials, such as protective clothing and ropes. The 1500d variant offers a balance of strength and weight, making it suitable for industrial applications and composites. The 3000d type, being thicker, is typically employed in heavy-duty applications, including aerospace and military gear, where durability is crucial.</p></p>
