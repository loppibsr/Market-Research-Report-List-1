<p><p>The ball segment valve market is segmented into two main types based on sealing mechanisms: static seals and inflatable seals. Static seals utilize a fixed gasket or O-ring to ensure a tight seal when the valve is closed, making them ideal for applications requiring high reliability and low leakage. In contrast, inflatable seals expand when inflated, creating a flexible and adaptable barrier that can accommodate variable tolerances and provide enhanced sealing capability in specialized applications. Both types serve critical roles in various industries.</p></p>
